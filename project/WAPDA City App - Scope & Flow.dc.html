01

Executive Summary

Why we are rebuilding, and what changes.

The first version of the WAPDA City app failed in adoption — residents found the interface complex, cluttered and confusing, and abandoned it. Version 2.0 treats simplicity as the primary requirement, not a nice-to-have. Every screen is designed for a non-technical audience spanning all age groups, including elderly and less-literate residents, using a large, icon-led, block-based layout with generous spacing and a calm, warm palette.

02

The Five User Roles

Each role has its own registration path and access level. Owners and family members share an identical in-app experience; tenants, employees and the MC differ.

ROLE 1 · CORE

Member (Resident / Owner)

The primary user — the registered property owner. Receives the full, unrestricted app experience including service-charge payment.

Registration

Select property number → OTP sent to the number registered against that property → sign up with name, email (both required) and profile picture (optional).

ROLE 2 · CORE

Family Member

A family member of a Member. Identical full app access to the owner. Distinguished only by their stated relationship.

Registration · relayed OTP

Enter property number → OTP is forwarded to the owner's registered mobile → the family member enters that OTP (read from the owner's phone) → fills name, email, mobile and relationship with the owner (brother / sister / son / etc.).

ROLE 3

Tenant

A resident renting the property. Full app access except service-charge challan payment and its payment record — every other feature is available.

Registration · relayed OTP

Enter property number → OTP forwarded to the owner's mobile → enter that OTP → fill name, mobile, email and picture (optional).

ROLE 4 · STAFF

WC Employee

Society staff with tiered access levels — some can only read & resolve complaints, others have broad access (but never financial challan submission). Employees scan visitor QR codes at gates, view visitor logs, read & resolve complaints, and view events with their attendee lists.

Registration

Enter HR ID / Employee ID → OTP sent to their registered mobile → access scoped to their assigned level.

ROLE 5 · ADMIN

MC (Management Committee)

The ~6 governing members of the society. The only role that can create events, polls, notices and notifications, and the only role that approves directory and labour listings.

  • View any resident's payment record
  • Read all complaints and change their status
  • Create & manage notices, events, polls, notifications
  • Add, view and remove any media
  • Create any visitor and browse the full visitor log with date/time filters
  • Approve Local Directory profiles & approve labour for the public Service-Providers directory
Common to all roles — the Profile section lets any user optionally add gender, date of birth and blood group. These are never mandatory at registration.

06

Feature Scope

Behaviour of each feature for residents, family members and tenants. MC and Employee variations are noted inline.

① Service Charges

Submit and view the current month's challan with a full cost breakdown by line item; view historical bills and reports; track paid / pending status. MC can view any resident's record. Tenants and employees cannot access challan payment or its record.

② Complaints

File a complaint by selecting a predefined type and predefined title, adding a description and attaching photos or a PDF. On submission a resolution timer starts and a status timeline tracks progress until addressed. Closure shows the resolver's remarks plus any attachment. Employees (by level) and MC read and change status.

③ Notices

Notices nag until read, shown with an unread / pending count. Users browse all notices and pinned notices; type is colour-coded — emergency, financial, entertainment, general. Created by MC only.

④ Events

The current date plus the next 6 days appear as a strip on top, with a full calendar for future events. Each event card shows a banner, time, date, venue, RSVP, and an attendee count with profile pictures (name on hover). Tapping a card opens details where the user marks going / not going, updating the live attendee count. Created by MC only; employees can view events and their attendee lists.

⑤ Media Hub

A repository of society office forms and PDFs (e.g. gas application, dispute form), event & announcement images, and YouTube video links to watch in-app. Managed (add / remove) by MC only.

⑥ Visitors

View today's visitor list with details, and an Invite Guest button opening a form:

  • Guest name · date & time (auto-picked) · gender (selectable)
  • Means of transport (bike / car / on foot) — if bike or car, ask number, colour, make
  • Duration of stay — hours (0–24) or days (0–5). Beyond 5 days, show: "Visit the society office for a permanent entry/exit pass."
  • Share via direct WhatsApp or a shareable PDF link; a QR is generated and passed on.

When the QR is scanned at the gate, an automated visitor entry is logged with the invitee's name, gender, address and house number. Employees scan; MC can create any visitor and view the full filtered log.

⑦ Community

A social feed of text or image posts. Others react (Facebook-style: like, love, sad, angry, wow) and comment. Posts rank by engagement, with filters for date, time, sort order (LIFO / FIFO) and category — Help, Entertainment, Announcement and more. Post type is defined on creation.

⑧ Polls

Browse past polls with results and MC remarks; vote in active polls (one vote per person). Opening and closing times and the running vote count are shown. Every poll states clearly: "Your vote is completely anonymous." Created by MC only.

⑨ Directory — two tabs

Local Directory lists resident experts (doctors, engineers, scientists, hakeems, etc.) with name, degree, years of experience, gender and a Contact button that opens in-app chat. A floating "Add yourself" button collects: display name, display contact, gender, area of expertise, sub-domain, experience, speciality, currently practising (y/n & where), best time to contact, off-hours, working hours, and a consent agreement to display.

Service Providers lists skilled labour by category (All, Electricians, Carpenters, Plumbers, Domestic Workers, Quran Teachers, Tutors…), searchable by name, type, rating and experience. A "List a worker" form captures display name, age, gender, experience, average cost, availability, the uploader's experience with them, and rating/review. Listings show hire count (5×, 10× badges), rating and feedback; tapping Contact reveals the number, counts the interaction, and shows how many times the worker has been called (phone icon).

Both Local Directory profiles and Service-Provider listings require MC approval before they appear publicly.

⑩ SOS

One tap surfaces the nearest security guard to call directly; the call is diverted to the society hotline for help. Always reachable from the centre of the bottom bar.

⑪ Me / Profile & Settings

Profile top shows counts: bills paid, complaints, events attended, pending notices. Below: name, address, role, member-since, gender, picture. Controls include notification enable/disable, settings, a Switch Panel button (for users holding multiple roles — e.g. an owner who is also an employee — to switch dashboards), and logout.

Settings: push notifications · SOS alerts · language (English / اردو) · dark & light mode · help & support · about & app version.

07

Design Principles

Minimal & uncluttered

Only top-priority actions on each screen. Whitespace is a feature, not wasted space.

Icon & block-based

Large tap targets with clear icons and labels, legible for elderly and less-literate residents.

Calm, warm palette

An elegant terracotta-and-cream system, easy on the eyes, with light & dark modes.

Subtle role cues

A light accent shift distinguishes Member/Family, Tenant, Employee and MC experiences — owners and family stay identical.

Open Questions for Sign-Off

Points to confirm with the client before build.

  1. Exact Employee access levels/tiers and what each may do (complaint-only vs. broad).
  2. Can Employees and MC post/comment in Community and vote in Polls, or only view? (shown as conditional above)
  3. In-app chat scope — directory contact only, or society-wide?
  4. Visitor pass beyond 5 days routes to the office — any in-app request, or fully manual?
  5. Payment gateways to support at launch (JazzCash, Easypaisa, card, bank transfer).
  6. Bottom-nav order confirmation: Media · Home · SOS · Community · Polls.
